# Business Domain Model

## Enterprise Business Operating System

Version: 1.0 (Architecture Blueprint)

---

## Purpose

This document defines the core business entities of the Enterprise Business Operating System.

It is a domain model, not an implementation plan. It describes the concepts the platform must preserve as it evolves from Luxury Mobility AI OS into a broader Business AI Operating System.

The model is aligned with:

- `docs/architecture/01_ENTERPRISE_BUSINESS_MODEL.md`
- `docs/architecture/04_CASE_ENGINE.md`
- `docs/architecture/12_OPERATIONS_CENTER.md`

The Case remains the operational center. AI Agents, Human Operators, messages, tasks, documents, intelligence, tool plans, decisions, outcomes, and experience all belong to business execution through Cases.

## Aggregate Ownership Model

The domain model is organized around aggregate roots. Aggregate roots define ownership boundaries, consistency boundaries, and the correct place to attach future database records.

The Case is the central operational aggregate. Company, Goal, Identity, Knowledge, Memory, Experience, and Policy are also important aggregate roots, but execution history belongs to the Case.

```text
Company
  owns Strategy
  owns Goals
  owns Business Capabilities
  owns Business Processes
  owns Policies
  owns Business Packs
  owns Knowledge
  owns Memory
  owns Experience
  owns Agents
  owns Human Operators

Goal
  owns goal definition
  owns success criteria
  creates / justifies Cases

Case
  owns Participants
  owns Messages
  owns Events
  owns Intelligence
  owns Decisions
  owns Tasks
  owns ToolPlans
  owns ToolRequests through ToolPlans
  owns Documents
  owns Outcome

Identity
  owns persistent identity data
  does not own Cases
  participates in Cases through Participants

Knowledge
  owns reusable reference information

Memory
  owns relationship and operational context

Experience
  owns company learning from closed Cases

Policy
  owns governance rules and constraints
```

### Main Aggregate Roots

#### Company Aggregate

The Company aggregate owns the operating system boundary. It owns strategy, goals, capabilities, processes, policies, business packs, knowledge, memory, experience, agents, and human operators. In future multi-tenant architecture, Company becomes the main isolation boundary.

#### Goal Aggregate

The Goal aggregate owns the business objective and success criteria. Goals create or justify Cases, but they do not own Case execution history.

#### Case Aggregate

The Case aggregate owns business execution. Participants, messages, events, intelligence, decisions, tasks, tool plans, tool requests through tool plans, documents, and outcome belong to the Case. Case is the central operational aggregate of the system.

#### Identity Aggregate

The Identity aggregate owns persistent identity data for people, organizations, systems, and agents. Identity does not own Cases. Identity participates in Cases through Participants.

#### Knowledge Aggregate

The Knowledge aggregate owns reusable reference information, source metadata, reliability level, categories, and version history. Knowledge can support many Cases but is not itself Case execution history.

#### Memory Aggregate

The Memory aggregate owns relationship and operational context. Memory may be linked to an Identity or to a Case, but it must remain distinct from Knowledge.

#### Experience Aggregate

The Experience aggregate owns company learning from closed Cases and Outcomes. Experience is private operational learning, not raw public knowledge.

#### Policy Aggregate

The Policy aggregate owns governance rules, constraints, escalation requirements, approval requirements, and safety boundaries. Policy governs all aggregates.

## Ownership Rules

- Case owns execution history.
- Event never exists outside Case.
- Message never exists outside Case.
- ToolRequest never executes outside Case.
- Intelligence belongs to Case.
- Decision belongs to Case.
- Outcome belongs to Case.
- Identity does not equal Participant.
- Agent participates in Case but never owns Case.
- Human Operator supervises Case but does not replace Case ownership.
- Knowledge is company-level unless explicitly linked to a Case.
- Memory may be Identity-level or Case-level but must not be mixed with Knowledge.
- Experience is created only from Outcomes / closed Cases.
- Policy governs all aggregates but does not belong to a single Case unless case-specific.

## 1. Company

### Definition

The Company is the top-level organizational entity that owns the operating system, its strategy, policies, cases, knowledge, memory, experience, operators, agents, and business packs.

### Purpose

To define the business boundary for ownership, governance, data isolation, policy enforcement, and accumulated experience.

### Owned By

- In single-company mode: the system owner.
- In future multi-tenant mode: the tenant or organization account.

### Owns

- Strategy
- Goals
- Business Capabilities
- Business Processes
- Cases
- Policies
- Knowledge
- Memory
- Experience
- Agents
- Human Operators
- Business Packs

### Related To

- Identity
- Participant
- Outcome
- Documents
- ToolPlans

### Must Not Be Confused With

- A customer company inside CRM.
- A participant company inside a Case.
- A legal entity used for yacht ownership, billing, escrow, or contracting.

## Single Source Of Truth Rules

1. The Case is the source of truth for business execution.
2. The Goal is the source of truth for why the Case exists.
3. The Identity is the source of truth for persistent people, organizations, systems, and agents.
4. The Participant is the source of truth for an Identity role inside a specific Case.
5. The Timeline/Event stream is the source of truth for what happened.
6. The Document record is the source of truth for document versions and review status.
7. The Knowledge record is the source of truth for reusable reference information.
8. The Memory record is the source of truth for relationship context.
9. The Intelligence record is the source of truth for AI analysis at a point in time.
10. The Decision record is the source of truth for accepted or reviewable judgment.
11. The ToolPlan and ToolRequest records are the source of truth for proposed operational actions.
12. The Outcome record is the source of truth for how the Case ended.
13. The Experience record is the source of truth for company learning from closed Cases.
14. Policies are the source of truth for what is allowed, blocked, escalated, or approval-gated.

---

## What Must Never Be Duplicated

- Do not duplicate Case state inside Messages, Tasks, Documents, or CRM records.
- Do not duplicate Identity data inside each Case; use Participants to reference Identities.
- Do not duplicate Participant permissions across unrelated entities; permissions should be derived from participant role and policy.
- Do not duplicate Message content as Intelligence; Intelligence may summarize or reference Messages.
- Do not duplicate ToolRequests as Tasks unless a Task is explicitly created from the ToolRequest.
- Do not duplicate Knowledge as Memory.
- Do not duplicate Memory as Knowledge.
- Do not duplicate Experience as public Knowledge without review and sanitization.
- Do not duplicate raw Documents as Knowledge; extract reviewed Knowledge entries instead.
- Do not duplicate Outcome as Case status; status tracks lifecycle, Outcome closes the Case.
- Do not duplicate Policy as prompt text only; prompts may reference Policy, but Policy must exist as its own governing entity.
- Do not duplicate Agent ownership of Cases; Agents participate, they do not own.
- Do not duplicate approval state across ToolRequest, Decision, and Task without explicit references.

- `agents`
- `human_operators`

Important design implications:

- `cases` should become the central join point for execution data.
- Most operational records should have `case_id` once Case Engine is implemented.
- `identity_id` should be separated from `participant_id`.
- `case_participants` should model role, permissions, relationship, and status for each Case.
- `events` should be append-only or treated as immutable.
- `intelligence_records` should preserve snapshots, not overwrite prior reasoning.
- `tool_requests` should preserve approval and execution history.
- `documents` should support versions and access controls.
- `knowledge_entries`, `memory_entries`, and `experience_entries` should remain separate because they answer different questions.
- `business_packs` should allow domain expansion without schema forks per vertical.
- Policy enforcement should be represented as data, not only code or prompts.

## What Is Intentionally Not Implemented Yet

The following are architecture concepts, not current implementation commitments:

- Full Case Engine database schema.
- Case creation workflow.
- Case Profiles as runtime-configurable modules.
- Multi-company / multi-tenant isolation.
- Identity graph.
- Participant permissions engine.
- Event-sourced timeline.
- Tool execution runtime.
- Autonomous tool execution.
- Business Pack marketplace.
- Workflow designer.
- Automation rules engine.
- Analytics and KPI layer.
- Experience Engine extraction pipeline.
- Policy engine as separate runtime service.
- Cross-company Cases.
- Linked Cases and Sub-Cases.
- Full document versioning and access control.
- Human assignment and team management.

Current implementation may contain early versions of agents, approvals, knowledge, memory, tasks, messages, and tool plans. Those are foundations, not the final Case Engine domain model.
